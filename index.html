01 / The Eduba frame

A methodology that plugs into what the academic partners already run.

The idea is simple. About 60% of any program's operations is database and code. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is where a real AI problem lives. Sorting those three layers before anything new is built is the work. Applied to a statewide veteran-entrepreneurship program, the scaffolding becomes a shared vocabulary with academic partners.

Cohort intake, academic-partner coordination, and outcome reporting to the Legislature are database and rule-based problems. AI-literacy content that keeps pace with what target industries actually hire for is the 10%, and it is the place where a methodology-first partner compounds beyond any single cohort.

The shape we would propose is a four-to-six-hour AI-orchestration module plugged into one cohort this fall, co-delivered with a lead academic partner. Measure, iterate, then scale.
